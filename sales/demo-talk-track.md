# CredTek — 20-Minute Demo Talk Track

The single most important sales asset. The demo decides 80% of close rate.
This is the live screen-share script — minute-by-minute, with screenshot cue
points, what to say, what to skip.

**Your goal in 20 minutes:** show the prospect their *own* future state running
on CredTek, get them excited enough to introduce a second decision-maker, and
end with a clear next step on the calendar.

**Your goal is NOT:** to walk through every feature. Most demos fail because
the seller tries to prove they built a lot of stuff. The buyer doesn't care
how much you built. They care whether you solve their specific pain.

---

## Pre-call (60 seconds before joining)

Have these tabs open in this order, left-to-right:

1. **`https://cred-tek.com`** — landing page, scrolled to the hero
2. **`https://cred-tek.com/dashboard`** — coordinator cockpit
3. **`https://cred-tek.com/providers/aisha-patel`** — supervision tracker
   (the differentiator)
4. **`https://cred-tek.com/licenses`** — multi-state matrix
5. **`https://cred-tek.com/payors`** — payor pipeline
6. **(Optional)** Their Modio screen if you got read-only access from a
   pre-call

Close every other tab. Mute Slack. If you screen-share a notification, you
look like a human; if you screen-share an unrelated work email, you look
unfocused.

## Minute 0–2 — Discovery, not pitching

Don't show your screen yet. Open camera-on, ask:

> "Before I show you anything, I want to make sure this is actually relevant.
> Can you walk me through how credentialing happens at [Group Name] today —
> from the moment you hire a provider to the day they're fully in-network?"

Listen. Don't interrupt. Take notes on:

- **Volume**: how many new providers per quarter
- **Time-to-active**: their current number (90? 120? 150?)
- **States**: how many states they operate in
- **Tech stack**: Modio? Spreadsheets? Outsourced CVO? Mix?
- **Pre-licensed providers**: do they have LMSWs/LPC-Associates/etc.?
- **Pain peaks**: which payor is the worst (Optum? Carelon? a state Medicaid?)
- **Recent triggers**: did Optum just demand delegated credentialing? Did
  they just lose a provider to a faster competitor?

Repeat their pain back to confirm:

> "OK, so just to make sure I heard you — your team of [N] is processing
> [X] new providers per quarter, you're averaging [Y] days to active, and
> the worst part of your week is [Z]. Is that right?"

Now you have the demo's golden thread. Every screen you show should map to
something they just said.

## Minute 2–4 — Hero + landing page

Share screen. Open the **landing page (tab 1)**.

> "Quick context on what CredTek is and isn't, then I'll get into your
> specific situation."

Scroll slowly past:

- Hero (`Get your behavioral health providers in-network in 45 days. Not 120.`)
- Pain agitation section (the six paraphrased VP quotes)
- "Why nothing has worked" (Modio, Symplr, Medallion cards)

Don't over-narrate. The page is dense and well-written; let it do the work.
Pause on the 6-quote pain section and say:

> "Did any of those sound familiar? Be honest."

Get them nodding. This is the agreement step. They have to verbally
acknowledge that you understand their world before they'll trust the demo.

Skip the rest of the landing page. Don't show pricing yet.

## Minute 8–13 — Aisha Patel (the supervision tracker)

This is the moment of the demo. Slow down.

> "Aisha is an LPC-Associate in Texas — pre-licensed. She's working toward
> her full LPC. To get there, Texas requires 3,000 supervised hours, of
> which 1,500 have to be direct client contact, with a licensed supervisor
> who's qualified to supervise under TX board rules.
>
> Today, in your group, this is probably tracked in a spreadsheet that
> Aisha's supervisor — or more likely the supervisor's assistant — updates
> every Friday."

Pause. Wait for them to confirm or share their version of this.

> "Here's how CredTek tracks it."

Scroll through the supervision card slowly:

- **The progress bar (1,840 / 3,000 hrs, 61% complete)** — "real-time
  against TX state board rules"
- **Supervisor + this week's cosignature** — "auto-reminder texts the
  supervisor when a cosignature is overdue"
- **Direct client contact / Group hours breakdown** — "TX requires both
  bucketed separately, so we track them separately"
- **Projected independent licensure date** — "this is auto-calculated
  based on current logging pace. Aisha hits LPC in September 2026 if she
  stays on track. Your finance team can model FTE forward off this."

Then the compliance check card:

> "And this is what your NCQA auditor sees. Every box green, every audit
> trail logged, every cosignature timestamped."

Land the line:

> "**No other credentialing tool does this for behavioral health.** Modio,
> Medallion, Symplr — they're built around the MD use case. Pre-licensed
> supervision is the thing every BH group has and every BH credentialing
> tool ignores. This is the feature that gets your team's vote."

## Minute 16–18 — The number

Switch to **tab 1 (landing page)**, scroll to the **Cost of Inaction**
section.

> "Quick math on whether this is worth doing. For a 200-provider group
> like yours, 75-day average idle time at $2,000/day per provider is
> roughly $6 million in lost annual revenue. Plus your coordinator team.
> Plus your outsourced CVO if you have one."

Scroll to the pricing section.

> "CredTek is $35 per provider per month plus $300 per enrollment action.
> For your group that's roughly **$96K a year** all-in. Versus $6 million
> in lost revenue. Payback is typically under 60 days."

Scroll to the **45-Day Guarantee**.

> "And we put real money behind it. If we don't get your average
> time-to-active under 60 days in the first 45 days, your subscription
> is refunded and we run your next 25 enrollments at no charge until
> we hit the number."

This is the reverse of the BANT objection — they don't have to talk
themselves into the spend; you've already made the math obvious.

## Minute 18–20 — Close

Don't ask "what do you think?" That's the worst close. Ask one of:

**A. The implementation question (signals serious interest):**
> "If we did this, who at [Group Name] would need to be in the room
> for the kickoff call?"

**B. The objection question (surfaces real concerns):**
> "What's the most uncomfortable thing about this for you right now?"

**C. The next-step question (locks in calendar time):**
> "I'd like to do a working session with [their Director of Credentialing
> + COO] where we walk through your specific provider list. Tuesday or
> Thursday next week?"

If you get ANY signal of interest, **lock the next meeting on their
calendar before you hang up**. Don't say "I'll send you a Cal link" —
open your shared calendar in the call and propose times.

## Common objections — how to handle live

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "We're already on Modio" | "Modio is great for tracking what's already credentialed. CredTek does the actual work — auto-enrollment with payors, pre-licensed supervision tracking, real PSV. Most of our customers run both for the first few months and then drop Modio." |
| "How fast can we go live?" | "14 days from contract to your first provider running through CredTek. We migrate your data from Modio or wherever, and your CSM is on weekly check-ins for 90 days." |
| "What about HIPAA / SOC 2 / NCQA?" | "HIPAA + signed BAA day one. SOC 2 Type II audit started at company founding. For NCQA, we partner with an NCQA-certified CVO and white-label their verifications — you get NCQA-compliant files immediately. We're pursuing our own NCQA CVO certification next." |
| "What if a payor's TOS prohibits automation?" | "Every submission gets coordinator approval before leaving CredTek. We're not scraping payors against their will — we're accelerating your team's manual work to seconds. For payors with direct enrollment APIs, we integrate natively." |
| "We need to think about it / talk to [boss]" | "Totally fair. Let's lock in a 30-min follow-up next week so I can answer their questions in real-time — what's better, [day A] or [day B]?" Always book the follow-up. Never let them "circle back." |

## Post-call — within 2 hours

Send this email. Speed matters.

```
Subject: CredTek follow-up — [Group Name]

[First Name] —

Three things from today:

1. Recap of what stuck out for you: [their #1 pain — the supervision
   tracker, the time-to-active number, whatever they reacted to most]

2. The next step we agreed: [calendar invite for [day], or "I'm sending
   over a sample contract for your legal team"]

3. The two links you asked for:
   - The 45-day guarantee in writing: [link to landing page #guarantee]
   - The pricing math for a [size] group: [calculator link or PDF]

Let me know if anything else comes up. I'll be ready to go end-to-end
on [date].

— [Your name]
```

## Recording demos for review

Record every demo (their consent: "do you mind if I record so I can
share the right parts with our build team?"). Watch the first 3 minutes
back the same day. Two weeks of disciplined recording will tell you
exactly which 30 seconds win or lose every demo.
