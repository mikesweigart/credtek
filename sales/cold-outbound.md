# CredTek — Cold Outbound Pack

Templates for the first 50 conversations. All copy is tuned for behavioral
health groups, 50–500 providers, multi-state, currently using Modio + spreadsheets
or an outsourced CVO. Replace `[bracketed]` fields before sending. Don't send
more than one channel per prospect per week — concurrent email + LinkedIn looks
desperate.

---

## A. Cold email — Director of Credentialing

**Subject lines (rotate, A/B test):**

- `quick question on your CAQH attestations`
- `[Group Name] credentialing — 45 days vs. 120`
- `the supervision tracker your team is asking for`

**Body:**

```
Hi [First Name],

Quick one — directors of credentialing at multi-state BH groups
your size keep telling me the same three things:

1. CAQH attestations are eating two days a week
2. Pre-licensed supervision tracking is in a spreadsheet that keeps
   breaking
3. New providers sit idle 90–120 days waiting on payor enrollment

We built CredTek specifically for this. AI agents that actually fill
the Optum/Carelon/Magellan applications (with your approval before
submit), state board PSV that doesn't break monthly, and the only
pre-licensed supervision tracker built for LCSW/LPC/LMFT that passes
NCQA audits.

45-day average time-to-active. 67% coordinator time saved. Or your
subscription is free for the first 90 days — that's our written
guarantee.

Worth 20 minutes? Here's my Cal link: [your-cal-link]

— [Your name]
[Your title], CredTek
cred-tek.com
```

**Why this works:**
- Specific pains in their words (CAQH attestations, supervision tracker
  spreadsheet, 90–120 days)
- Names competitors implicitly (Optum/Carelon/Magellan = BH payor
  specialization, signals you understand their world)
- Risk reversal in the close (free if we don't perform)
- Self-serve CTA — no email volley

## B. Cold email — COO at a PE-backed BH rollup

PE-backed COOs care about EBITDA timing, not workflow ergonomics.
Different message.

**Subject lines:**

- `time-to-revenue per new provider — [Group Name]`
- `the credentialing math your CFO actually cares about`

**Body:**

```
[First Name] —

If you're onboarding 8–15 new providers per quarter and your average
time-to-active is north of 75 days, you're leaving roughly $1.5–$2.25M
on the table per year in delayed revenue. That math gets worse every
quarter you grow.

CredTek closes the gap. AI agent stack purpose-built for behavioral
health: Optum/Carelon/Magellan auto-enrollment, real state-board PSV,
pre-licensed supervision tracking, NCQA-compliant audit binder.

At $35/provider/month + $300/enrollment, payback is typically under
60 days against the lost revenue alone — before we count what you
save on coordinator headcount.

20 minutes, demo straight on your providers? [your-cal-link]

— [Your name]
```

**Why this works:**
- Opens with money, not workflow
- Specific dollar range with the math implied
- Payback period calc is the line CFOs forward to their boss
- No fluff, single CTA

## C. Warm intro follow-up

When a partner makes the intro, hit reply within 4 hours. Speed of
follow-up is the strongest predictor of close rate on warm intros.

```
[First Name] — thanks [Partner Name] for the intro.

[Partner Name] mentioned [specific context they shared, e.g. "you
just expanded into three new states and are trying to figure out
the licensing piece" or "your supervision tracking is in a
spreadsheet"]. That's exactly what we built CredTek for.

Two ways to do this — you pick:

1. 20 min on Cal: [your-cal-link]
2. I send you a 3-min Loom walking through your specific situation
   so you can decide if a live demo is worth it

Either way, no slides. We pull a sample of your providers and run
them through CredTek live.

— [Your name]
```

## D. Follow-up after no response (day 5)

```
[First Name] — circling back on this. If credentialing isn't a
priority right now, no worries — happy to disappear.

If it IS but the timing is bad, just reply "next quarter" and I'll
ping you in 90 days.

— [Your name]
```

**Send rate:** ~30% of cold prospects who ignore the first email
respond to this one. The "give them an out" framing reduces guilt
about not responding.

## Cadence

A reasonable outbound cadence per prospect:

| Day | Channel | Template |
|-----|---------|----------|
| 0   | Email   | A or B (depending on title) |
| 3   | LinkedIn connection request | F |
| 5   | Email follow-up | D |
| 8   | LinkedIn DM (if connected) | E |
| 14  | Final email | (custom — reference something specific about their group) |

Stop after day 14 if no response. Re-add to the list 90 days later
with new context (something they posted on LinkedIn, a press release,
a hiring announcement).

---

## Subject line tips

- Lowercase subject lines outperform Title Case 3:1 in B2B outbound
- One specific word from their world ("CAQH", "Optum delegation",
  "PSYPACT", "supervision") doubles open rates
- Avoid: "quick chat?", "[Company] x CredTek", anything with "synergy"
